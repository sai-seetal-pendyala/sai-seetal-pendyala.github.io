ABOUT

I never had a plan. I just knew I wanted to wake up every day and do something I couldn't stop thinking about.

That instinct pulled me into design. Design taught me how the way something looks, shapes the way people feel and decide. Two years in it, I got good at reading that invisible layer most people walk past. But the most interesting design decisions were never the prettiest ones. They were the ones backed by something real. Why does this actually work? Who is it really for? I started asking questions design couldn't answer on its own. And I realized that was the most interesting problem in the room.

That's what brought me to analytics. Behind every campaign, every click, every decision a person makes without quite knowing why, there's a story buried in the data. I spend my days building models, running experiments, untangling the why behind what the numbers are showing. Because numbers are just how that story confesses itself. And I get to be the one who makes the numbers speak.

When I'm not buried in data, I'm playing table tennis, in the kitchen experimenting with something I saw at 2am, or strumming a ukulele chord I just learned. I sing when nobody's listening. And whenever I can, I'm out somewhere new, because the best ideas have always come from places I didn't plan to end up.
